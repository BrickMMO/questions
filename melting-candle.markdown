## melting candle

### There are two candles of equal lengths but different thickness. The thicker candle lasts for 6 hours , while the thinner lasts for 2 hours less than the thicker one . A person lights the two candles at the same time and goes to play. After returning home, the person saw that the thicker candle was twice the length of thinner one.

### How long ago did the person light the two candles?

**Solution: 3**

The thicker candle has a total burning time of 6 hours, so in one hour it burns 1/6 of its total length. Therefore, after hours, the portion of the candle that has burned is t/6, and the remaining length is (6 − t)/6.

Similarly, the thinner candle has a total burning time of 4 hours, so it burns 1/4 of its length per hour. After t hours, the portion burned is t/4, and the remaining length is (4 − t)/4.

To understand how values like 4/6 and 3/6 are obtained, we observe the thicker candle over time:

After 2 hours, it has burned 2 × (1/6) = 2/6, so the remaining length is 6/6 − 2/6 = 4/6.
After 3 hours, it has burned 3 × (1/6) = 3/6, so the remaining length is 6/6 − 3/6 = 3/6.
Thus, these fractions represent the remaining portion of the candle after subtracting the burned part from the original full length.
